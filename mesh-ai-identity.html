A logo should do one thing before anything else: explain the product without a single word of copy. This one does.

01 · The geometry
Six nodes, one hub. That’s the product.
The ring maps directly to the architecture. Six agents, six nodes. Everything connects back to the blue center, which is the human. The HR lead, the payroll manager, the employee. They’re not replaced or automated around. They’re the whole point.
02 · The animation
The logo is a product demo.
Lines come in from the outer nodes. Never more than three at once, which mirrors how the product actually works. Each active dot lights up in its agent’s color. The hub responds as input lands. When the work settles, three connections hold and the name appears. Watch the logo move and you’ve seen the product run.
03 · The feel
Nothing to figure out.
Clean enough to get out of the way. That’s what this product needs to feel like for the admin running payroll on a Friday afternoon. No friction, no noise, just clear execution. The mark reflects that from the first second you see it.
04 · The system
One mark. Six identities.
Every agent carries the same geometry in their own color. Penny is indigo. Riley is amber. Milo is emerald. When an agent is working, their mark lights up. One mark does the job of an entire icon family and every piece stays on-brand without any extra work.
