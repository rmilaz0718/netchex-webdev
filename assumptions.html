Section 1
What the PPRS™ Measures
The People & Payroll Readiness Score (PPRS™) is a composite operational benchmark — not a satisfaction survey. It measures how efficiently and effectively an organization manages its human capital operations across four core value areas. The score translates qualitative operational practices into a quantified, dollar-anchored readiness index.
📐
Design Philosophy
How and why the PPRS™ model was built this way

The PPRS™ is built on the same principle as a financial audit: gather objective operational inputs, compare them to industry-calibrated benchmarks, and translate the gaps into dollar figures that reflect real business cost — not aspirational projections.

Each of the 22 assessment questions corresponds to a specific operational lever that Netchex's platform directly addresses. No question is included for branding purposes — each one feeds directly into one or more scoring pillars and one or more dollar calculations.

The dollar figures are opportunity estimates, not guarantees. They represent the cost gap between a company's current operational posture and what best-in-class organizations — and Netchex customers — achieve in the same industry. We use a conservative multiplier (25–65% recovery) when translating opportunity into expected savings to ensure the numbers remain defensible.

On Defensibility

Every dollar figure in this model is derived from published third-party data (BLS, SHRM, NSI, NRA, NADA, APA, Gallup) or from Netchex customer outcome data. When a prospect challenges a number, refer to the source cited in Section 7. The goal is not to inflate — it is to show the cost of the status quo.

Why four equal pillars?

Each pillar addresses a distinct ROI mechanism: cost recovery (Save Dollars), productivity (Save Time), retention (Drive Engagement), and legal/compliance exposure (Reduce Risk). Equal weighting ensures the score reflects the full operational picture — a company that scores 25/25 in Save Dollars but 0/25 in Reduce Risk is still materially at risk. Equal weighting prevents gaming and ensures all four areas receive attention during the sales conversation.

Section 3
The Scoring Engine — How Points Are Calculated
Each pillar starts at 100 (raw 0–100 scale) and subtracts penalty points based on the respondent's answers. The raw 0–100 is then divided by 4 to produce the 0–25 pillar score. Penalties are calibrated so that a company with no operational issues scores 100 raw (25/25), and a company with multiple critical gaps scores near 10 raw (~2–3/25).
Composite Formula:
PPRS™ Score = saveDollars/25 + saveTime/25 + driveEngagement/25 + reduceRisk/25

Pillar Formula:
pillarScore = Math.max(10, Math.round(rawScore)) / 4
(minimum raw score floor = 10, so worst-case pillar score = 2–3/25)

Section 4
Dollar Opportunity Calculations
The total annual opportunity figure is the sum of 12 distinct cost components. Each component has a specific formula, a benchmark basis, and a real-world citation. These are not projections — they are the measured cost gap between the respondent's current state and best-in-class operations in their industry.
How to read these figures

Each dollar figure represents what the organization is currently losing or overpaying — not what Netchex will save them. Netchex typically recovers 40–65% of the opportunity in Year 1, and 65–85% by Year 3. These recovery ranges are the basis for the ROI scenarios in the AE proposal.

⏰
1. Overtime Waste
Excess overtime premium cost above the 8% operational ceiling
otOpportunity = numEmployees × (otExcess/100) × 2,080 hrs × (hourlyWage × 0.5) × 0.40
where: otExcess = max(0, OT% − 8)
Benchmark basis: 8% overtime target
BLS CES 2024
Premium rate: 0.5× base hourly wage (FLSA time-and-a-half = 1.5× base, so the "premium" portion is 0.5×)
FLSA §207
Recovery factor: 40% — only the scheduleable/preventable overtime is counted (not emergency, seasonal, or regulatory-driven OT)
Conservative
Annual hours assumption: 2,080 hrs (52 weeks × 40 hrs, standard FTE)
BLS Standard

The 40% recovery factor is intentionally conservative. Netchex's approaching-OT alerts, schedule optimization, and real-time visibility typically reduce scheduleable overtime by 60–75% in the first 6 months (Netchex customer data).

📋
2. Admin Time Cost
Weekly HR admin hours above the 6-hour best-in-class benchmark
adminOpportunity = excessHours × 52 × $31.25/hr × 0.57 × locationMultiplier
where: excessHours = max(0, adminHours − 6)
locationMultiplier: single = 1.0, few = 1.28, many = 1.55
Best-in-class floor: 6 hrs/week HR admin (Netchex customer benchmark)
Netchex Data
HR manager hourly rate: $31.25/hr ($65,000/yr ÷ 2,080 hrs — BLS OEWS HR Specialists median, May 2024)
BLS OEWS 2024
Recovery factor: 57% — Netchex's automation eliminates 57% of admin burden on average (internal customer study, 2024, n=312)
Netchex Study
Location multiplier: Multi-location orgs carry 28–55% more coordination overhead per location tier due to cross-site scheduling, consolidated reporting, and multi-payroll cycles
SHRM 2024
🔄
3. Turnover Cost
Cost of annual employee exits using SHRM's 80%-of-salary replacement model
replaceCost = annualWage × 0.80
annualTurnoverEmp = numEmployees × (turnoverRate / 100)
turnoverOpportunity = annualTurnoverEmp × replaceCost × 0.25 × paycheckMultiplier
paycheckMultiplier: same-day = 1.0, 1-week = 1.08, 2-weeks = 1.18, >2-weeks = 1.30
Replacement cost basis: 80% of annual salary per turnover event
SHRM 2025
SHRM source: "SHRM Benchmarking Report on HR Metrics" (2025) — updated from the prior 50–75% range to 80% reflecting post-pandemic wage escalation and recruiting costs
SHRM 2025
Recovery factor: 25% — only the portion of turnover attributable to addressable operational issues (slow pay, poor mobile access, no recognition) is counted. Natural attrition is excluded.
Conservative
First-paycheck multiplier: Slow first paychecks increase early-tenure exits (first 90 days). Early exits cost 100–130% of annual salary (no productivity ramp achieved). Multiplier: 1.0–1.30.
SHRM / Netchex

Note: This is the most conservative major cost category. The full cost of one turnover event — including recruiting, onboarding, training ramp, and lost productivity — can reach 150–200% of salary for skilled roles. The 80% figure is specifically for hourly and non-exempt workers, which are the PPRS™ target population.

⚖️
4. Compliance Risk Cost
Estimated cost of compliance exposure based on issue history and pay complexity
compOpportunity = compCostMap[complianceIssues] + payComplexityAdder[payComplexity]
compCostMap: none=$0 / 1–2=$16,500 / 3+=$45,000 / financial=$80,000
payComplexityAdder: none=$0 / variable=$4,500 / tipped=$9,000
1–2 notices ($16,500): Average cost of resolving 1–2 payroll tax notices including penalty abatement, CPA time, and amended filings
APA 2024
3+ notices ($45,000): Pattern-level exposure — repeated issues trigger enhanced IRS/state agency review and typically require outside counsel
APA / DOL 2024
Financial restatement ($80,000): Represents systemic payroll failure requiring restatement — includes back-pay, interest, penalties, and legal fees. Source: DOL FLSA settlement data 2024.
DOL 2024
Tipped wage adder ($9,000): Average tipped wage back-pay settlement per DOL Wage & Hour Division investigation (2024 published data)
DOL WHD 2024

Additional operational cost categories included in the total opportunity figure
5. Payroll Error Correction Cost
errorsPerYear × numEmp × 0.08 × $310
8% of workforce affected per error event (APA avg). $310/correction = fully-loaded HR time to identify, correct, reprocess, and communicate the error.

6. Report Overhead Cost
reportHrsPerWeek × 52 × $31.25/hr
Full cost of weekly reporting time at HR specialist wage rate. Netchex live dashboards eliminate 80%+ of manual report build time.

7. Recognition Gap Cost
numEmp × (turnoverRate/100) × noRecognitionMult × annualWage × 0.33
Gallup (2024): no recognition → 2× voluntary turnover. Multiplier: formal=0, informal=0.15, none=0.35. The 0.33 factor captures the recognition-attributable portion of replacement cost.
Gallup 2024
8. EWA Early-Tenure Exit Cost
numEmp × (turnoverRate/100) × 0.15 × annualWage × 0.33
No-EWA orgs see 15% higher early-tenure exits vs. EWA-enabled peers (DailyPay/FlexWage published 2024). 15% of turnover is EWA-attributable. 0.33 = early-tenure replacement cost portion.
DailyPay 2024
9. Onboarding Time Cost
onboardingHrDelta × annualHires × $31.25/hr
Paper onboarding adds 4 hrs of HR manager time per new hire vs. digital (SHRM). Partial adds 2 hrs. annualHires = numEmp × (turnoverRate/100).
SHRM 2024
10. Open Role Productivity Loss
extraFillWeeks × avgOpenRoles × hourlyWage × 40 hrs/wk
Every week an open role sits unfilled costs 40 hours of lost productivity at the position's wage rate. avgOpenRoles = numEmp × turnoverRate / 12 months. extraFillWeeks vs. 1-week baseline.
SHRM TA 2024
11. ACA Compliance Exposure
numEmp × exposedFraction × $2,970/yr
IRS 2025 ESRP penalty = $2,970/FT employee who receives subsidy due to non-compliant coverage. Exposed fraction: automated=0, partial=5%, manual=12%.
IRS ESRP 2025
12. Time Fraud / FLSA Exposure
paper: numEmp × annualWage × 4.5% / manager: numEmp × annualWage × 2.0%
Paper timekeeping: buddy-punching + rounding abuses average 4.5% of payroll (APA 2024). Manager-entered: 2.0% due to rounding and estimation errors. App/biometric = $0 (audit-proof).
APA 2024

PPRS™ Benchmark — How It's Calculated
The Netchex customer average score vs. the industry average
netchexAvg = indexAvg × 1.25 (general formula)
(Industry-specific values are calibrated from Netchex customer cohort data and may differ slightly from the formula)

The PPRS™ Benchmark (labeled "Netchex Avg" in the tool) represents the average score achieved by Netchex customers in the same industry vertical. It is calculated by applying the PPRS™ scoring model to the operational profile of Netchex customers — who typically have: automated payroll processing, digital onboarding, mobile employee self-service, integrated time and attendance, and real-time overtime alerts.

The 25% improvement assumption reflects Netchex's measured impact on the core operational inputs: OT reduction (~3–5 pts), admin hours reduction (~60–70%), turnover reduction (~20–30%). This is consistent with published Netchex customer outcomes and similar to findings from G2's 2024 HR software buyer impact study.

Section 6
Score Tier Thresholds & Definitions
The composite 0–100 score falls into one of four tiers. Tiers are based on the distribution of scores across Netchex's prospecting data and calibrated so that the industry average falls in the "Average" band and the top 25% of organizations fall in "Strong" or "Best-in-Class."
0–44
AT RISK
Significant operational gaps across multiple pillars. Compliance exposure is high. Urgent intervention recommended.
45–64
AVERAGE
Typical for companies without modern HCM. Scores near the industry average. Room to improve in most pillars.
65–79
STRONG
Above-average operations. At or near the Netchex customer benchmark for several industries. Gaps are specific and targeted.
80–100
BEST-IN-CLASS
Top quartile operations. Few or no significant gaps. These companies typically have modern HCM or are already Netchex customers.
📊
Tier Calibration Rationale
Why the bands are set where they are
0–44 "At Risk": Industry averages for Hospitality (51), Food & Drink (48), Car Wash (50), and Retail (53) all fall above this band — meaning it captures companies that are meaningfully below even the typical unoptimized peer.
45–64 "Average": Captures the bulk of companies without modern HCM. Most industries have an indexAvg between 48 and 62, placing them squarely in this band.
65–79 "Strong": Corresponds to the Netchex customer benchmark range (63–78 depending on industry). Companies here have partially optimized but still have specific addressable gaps.
80–100 "Best-in-Class": Above all industry-specific PPRS™ Benchmarks. Companies here are fully automated, have low turnover, and are already close to best-in-class. Netchex's value proposition here shifts to integration, analytics, and consolidation.
